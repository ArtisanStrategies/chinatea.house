# Niche Brief: chinatea.house

## The Opportunity

**Domain:** Chinese tea (including Taiwanese tea traditions)

**Core thesis:** No comprehensive, well-organized, English-language resource exists for Chinese tea. Current information is fragmented across vendor blogs, Reddit threads, and niche enthusiast sites. We will build the definitive reference.

**Target audience:**
- Primary: Tea-curious beginners seeking guidance (highest volume)
- Secondary: Intermediate enthusiasts deepening knowledge (high engagement)
- Tertiary: Connoisseurs seeking specific information (low volume, high trust)

---

## Data Foundation

**Primary dimensions:**

| Dimension | Estimated Entities | Source |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| Tea categories | 7 (green, white, yellow, oolong, black, dark, scented) | Manual curation |
| Specific tea varieties | 500-800 | Vendor catalogs, Babelcarp, Wikipedia |
| Growing regions | 200-300 (province → prefecture → mountain → village) | Government data, vendor info |
| Preparation methods | 10-15 | Manual curation |
| Teaware types | 50-100 | Vendor catalogs |
| Flavor descriptors | 50-100 | Tasting wheel research |
| Occasions/use cases | 30-50 | Keyword research |
| Health benefits | 20-30 | Academic sources |

**Combinatorial page generation:**
- [Tea] brewing guide: 600 × 5 methods = 3,000
- [Tea A] vs [Tea B]: ~15,000 sensible pairings
- Best tea for [occasion]: 600 × 40 = 24,000
- [Tea] from [region]: ~5,000 relevant combinations
- [Tea] + [modifier] (caffeine, benefits, price): 600 × 10 = 6,000

**Total addressable pages:** 50,000 - 150,000+

## Keyword Taxonomy

### Head terms (pillar pages)
- "[category] tea guide" — pu-erh tea guide, oolong tea guide
- "best [category] tea" — best green tea, best pu-erh tea
- "[region] tea" — yunnan tea, fujian tea, wuyi tea
- "how to brew [category]" — how to brew pu-erh
- "chinese tea for beginners"
- "gongfu tea ceremony"

### Long-tail terms (satellite pages)
- "[tea] vs [tea]" — tieguanyin vs da hong pao
- "[tea] brewing temperature/time"
- "[tea] caffeine content"
- "[tea] health benefits"
- "best tea for [occasion]" — best tea for focus, best tea for sleep
- "[tea] review"
- "how to store [tea]"
- "[teaware] for [tea type]"

### Modifiers that multiply
- Experience level: beginner, advanced
- Price tier: budget, premium, investment-grade
- Time of day: morning, afternoon, evening
- Season: spring, autumn, winter
- Brewing style: gongfu, western, grandpa, cold brew
- Age: young, aged, vintage

## Monetization Path

**Phase 1 (Months 1-6):** Traffic building
- No monetization
- Focus on indexation and organic growth
- Build email list via content upgrades

**Phase 2 (Months 6-12):** Digital products
- Brewing guides (PDF)
- Tasting journal templates
- Email course: "30 Days of Chinese Tea"
- Potential: Video courses on gongfu brewing

**Phase 3 (Year 2+):** E-commerce
- Curated tea selections
- Teaware (gaiwans, yixing, accessories)
- Subscription boxes
- Private label potential

**Architecture implications:**
- Email capture slots in templates from day 1
- Product database schema designed for future e-commerce
- "Where to buy" sections ready for affiliate → owned transition
